Work About Resume

TAC X RETRO

OVERVIEW: Thornhill Athletic Council (TAC) is a student-run council that works directly with Thornhill Secondary School (TSS) to support funding healthy active living in the community and help fund TSS’s athletic program.

TASK: As a Co-Director of Fundraising, I had to think of a creative way to get rid of a lot of sports wear that were no longer in use. After discussing with the teacher advisors, we settled on doing a silent auction for the garments during lunch periods over the course of several days. The only question now was how to promote old sports wear to the students of TSS.

PLAN: Photograph TAC members modelling the garments and produce a promotional video for the fundraiser.

Process

1. Storyboarding

When the teacher advisors first proposed this idea, the first thing that popped into my head was to campaign this fundraiser as a “retro sale”. Vintage fashion was rising in public interest at the time, and the fact that the garments we were selling were authentic vintage pieces, the theme and product fit together perfectly. After looking at all the garments in our iventory, I created a mood board to reflect the vision I had for this campaign and headlined this project as TAC X RETRO.

2. Execution

To really hit that relatability factor and resonate with the student body, I asked TAC members to model the garments that we were selling. I wanted to do my best to make the photos to look as professional as possible, so I took a photography crash course from the friend who lent me her camera. With my limited experience as a photographer and the help from the TAC graphics department on set, we pulled together a two-day shoot so the photographs could get edited and posted in time for the fundraiser.

3. Post-production

The photos were a challenge to edit since there were several different locations each sporting different lighting. Due to this, I opted to pair together photos with smiliar ambient lighting so the overall image looks cohesive. As for the layout, I was inspired by various fashion retailers and how they list their products on their Instagram, so I included the garment and model names in some white space next to the corresponding photo.

Final Result

The fundraiser was an overwhelming success, with the Instagram posts of this campaign garnering an average of 115 likes per post and selling over 50 garments. This campaign was definately one of the highlights of my senior year in high school and while it came with its fair share of challenges, I had a lot of fun working with my talented council members! Feel free to check out the @tssathleticcouncil Instagram account for all the photos and the promotional video!
